Network Topologies and Transport Architectures

Simple Physical Topologies

    Physical topology refers to the physical arrangement of nodes in a network. There are four basic shapes:
    • Bus
    • Ring
    • Mesh
    • Star

Why do we need a topology?

Choosing one topology over another can have an effect on:

  1. Kind of network equipment required
  2. Capabilities of network equipment
  3. Network growth
  4. Method of network management

Simple Physical Bus Topologies

A Bus topology is made up of a single cable, known as a backbone, that connects all nodes on a network without the use of any intermediary connectivity devices.

Advantages
    • It is suitable for small networks.
    • Simple to set up
    • Implementation is relatively inexpensive
